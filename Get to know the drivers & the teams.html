McLaren is a British Formula 1 team founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren. They entered F1 in 1966 and became one of the sport’s most successful teams, especially during the 1980s and 90s with stars like Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost. McLaren has won 8 Constructors' and 12 Drivers' Championships, with champions like Mika Häkkinen and Lewis Hamilton.

After a tough period in the 2010s, McLaren has bounced back with rising stars like Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri, and is once again competing near the front.

Ferrari

Ferrari is the oldest and most successful team in F1, founded in 1929 by Enzo Ferrari. They joined Formula 1 in 1950 and have since won 16 Constructors' Championships and 15 Drivers' Championships. Ferrari's legendary drivers include Juan Manuel Fangio, Niki Lauda, Michael Schumacher, and more recently, Sebastian Vettel and Charles Leclerc.

Schumacher led Ferrari to a dominant era in the early 2000s, winning five consecutive titles. While they've faced challenges in recent years, Ferrari remains one of the sport's most iconic and competitive teams.

Red Bull Racing

Teams
Added by
Balan Ana

Red Bull Racing entered Formula 1 in 2005 after purchasing the Jaguar team. They quickly became a powerhouse, winning 4 consecutive Constructors' Championships and 4 Drivers' Championships with Sebastian Vettel from 2010 to 2013. Their dominance was driven by strong engineering, notably their innovative RB series cars, and their partnership with Renault engines.

In recent years, Red Bull has remained a top team, with Max Verstappen leading the charge. Verstappen won the 2021 and 2022 Drivers' Championships, and the team claimed the 2022 Constructors' Championship, showcasing their ongoing strength.

Mercedes

Mercedes has been a dominant force in Formula 1, especially since their re-entry as a works team in 2010. However, their true success came in the hybrid era, starting in 2014, when they won 8 consecutive Constructors' Championships (2014–2020) and 7 Drivers' Championships with Lewis Hamilton. Hamilton's partnership with Mercedes cemented their status as the team to beat, with incredible performance and reliability.

Mercedes has been a pioneer in hybrid engine technology, and their dominance was built on solid engineering and smart strategy. In recent years, they’ve faced stiffer competition from teams like Red Bull, but they remain one of the most respected teams in F1, with Hamilton and George Russell driving for them.

Aston Martin returned to Formula 1 as a works team in 2021, after buying the Racing Point team, which was previously known as Force India. The team’s roots in F1 date back to 1959, but their most recent success has been driven by the Lawrence Stroll-led ownership and a heavy investment in car development. The team rebranded as Aston Martin to leverage the luxury sports car brand’s prestige.

In 2023, Aston Martin saw a significant boost in performance with their new car design, emerging as strong contenders, especially with Fernando Alonso driving for them. Their recent results and rapid development have made them one of the most exciting teams in F1, aiming to challenge the established powerhouses like Mercedes, Red Bull, and Ferrari.

Alpine is the French team that replaced Renault in Formula 1 in 2021 as part of a rebranding effort. Renault had been a prominent F1 team since the mid-2000s, with notable successes, including two Drivers' Championships in 2005 and 2006 with Fernando Alonso.

Alpine has consistently been in the midfield, with their best recent result being Esteban Ocon’s win at the 2021 Hungarian Grand Prix. The team has been focused on improving its performance and challenging the top teams, often relying on strong driver performances, including Fernando Alonso (who returned to Alpine in 2021) and Esteban Ocon.

Haas F1 Team is an American-owned team that debuted in Formula 1 in 2016. Founded by Gene Haas, the team quickly became one of the more competitive midfield teams, benefiting from technical partnerships with Ferrari for their engines and other components. Haas' first season saw them scoring points right away, a strong start for a new team.

The team has struggled to maintain consistency over the years, but they have occasionally shown promising performance, especially in 2018 when they finished 5th in the Constructors' Championship.In recent years, the team has had a more challenging time competing at the front, with Mick Schumacher and Kevin Magnussen (who returned to the team in 2022) leading the charge. Haas has been working to improve its car and overall performance, hoping to return to more regular points finishes.

Racing Bulls, formerly known as Toro Rosso and AlphaTauri, is Red Bull Racing's sister team, based in Faenza, Italy. The team was originally Minardi, founded in 1985, and was acquired by Red Bull in 2005. It serves as a platform for developing young drivers before they move to the senior Red Bull team.

acing Bulls has produced notable drivers like Sebastian Vettel, who won his first race with them in 2008, and Pierre Gasly, who won in 2020. The team operates with technical support from Red Bull Racing, using similar components and chassis designs, which helps them stay competitive in the midfield.

Williams is a legendary F1 team founded in 1977 by Frank Williams. They were highly successful in the 1980s and 1990s, winning 9 Constructors' Championships and 7 Drivers' Championships with drivers like Nigel Mansell and Damon Hill.

However, the team struggled in the 2000s and faced financial difficulties. In 2020, Williams was bought by Dorilton Capital and has since focused on rebuilding. While still a midfield team, they’ve shown signs of improvement with drivers like George Russell.

Sauber is a Swiss motorsport team that has been a part of Formula 1 since its debut in 1993. Founded by Peter Sauber, the team initially operated under its own name before being acquired by BMW in 2005, becoming BMW Sauber. The team enjoyed some success during this period, including a second-place finish in the 2008 Constructors' Championship.

In 2009, BMW pulled out of F1, and Sauber rebranded back to its original name. Over the years, the team has seen varying levels of success, often struggling in the midfield, but occasionally showing promise with strong performances. In 2018, Sauber entered a new era after a partnership with Alfa Romeo, becoming known as Alfa Romeo Racing
